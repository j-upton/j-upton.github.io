Hello!

Call me Jacob, or rather don't. My parents didn't, or nearly didn't—for they
named me Jake, and that is close enough for some.
They said to me, "we named you… what we planned to call you."
They created from my name a pit fall
for the presumptuous.
Some call me ចេកចៀន. Nobody calls me Οὔτις. One calls me bụng bia.

My name is Jake Upton and I am a first-year English master's student at Boston College. I am engaged in the study of Black speculative fiction, and according to many notebooks and first drafts, I am a creative writer. I discovered my love of teaching in Cambodia, where I taught English for two years in the Peace Corps. At Boston College I am currently working as a Writing Fellow, as a Multi-Language Learner Writing Specialist, and as a Writing Tutor at the Connors Family Learning Center.

In addition to creative writing, my hobbies include martial arts (wrestling, Taekwondo, Bokator, HEMA), language learning, wilderness survival, running, and skiing.
